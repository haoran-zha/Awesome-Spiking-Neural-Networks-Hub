% Abstract
Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) are the third generation of neural network
models, drawing inspiration from the event-driven, sparse, and temporally
dynamic computation observed in biological brains. This survey provides a
comprehensive overview of SNNs, covering their biological foundations, neuron
models, encoding schemes, training methodologies (including ANN-to-SNN
conversion, surrogate-gradient backpropagation, and biologically plausible
learning rules), neuromorphic hardware platforms, and application domains.
We further discuss open challenges and promising research directions, aiming
to serve as an accessible entry point for newcomers and a reference for
researchers in the field.
